# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The owner (sole editor) and his brother (view-only), plus friends he shares the link with.
Fragrance enthusiasts who know these scents. Context: on a phone deciding what to wear, or
browsing the collection on desktop. The job: find a fragrance fast — by mood, season,
occasion, house, or family — and see what it is, how it wears, and what it layers with.

## Product Purpose

A personal archive of 313 fragrances (mostly clones/"impressions" of designer & niche
originals) made browsable, scannable, and shareable. Success = the owner can answer "what
should I wear?" in seconds and the brother can explore the whole collection on his phone
without help.

## Brand Personality

Premium, tactile, collector's-cabinet. Three words: **rich, legible, confident.** It should
feel like browsing a beautifully organized fragrance shelf — colorful and alive, never
clinical, never a boring spreadsheet.

## Anti-references

- The previous light, low-contrast, beige look — too pale, hard to scan, "boring."
- Generic SaaS dashboards; flat gray admin tables.
- Cream/paper warm-neutral AI default.

## Design Principles

1. **Color is the index.** The 9 fragrance families are the primary visual system — you
   should navigate the collection by color before reading a word.
2. **Scannable in one pass.** Strong hierarchy, grouped sections, generous but rhythmic
   spacing. A 313-item collection must never feel like a wall.
3. **Legible first.** Rich color never costs contrast; body and labels stay ≥4.5:1 in both
   themes.
4. **Two real themes.** Dark is the default and the showcase; light is a first-class
   alternative, not an afterthought.
5. **Owner edits, brother views.** Keep the collaboration seam intact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Verified contrast in both themes. Full keyboard nav + visible focus. Respect
`prefers-reduced-motion`. Family color never the sole signal — always paired with a label.
